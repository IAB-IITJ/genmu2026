Genμ 2.0 • 2026 edition

Multimodal Identity Unlearning Challenge

The 2026 update expands the original Genμ concept-unlearning benchmark into a broader privacy-preserving suite. The legacy challenge remains as Task 3, while two new tracks focus on forgetting identities in face generation and speaker generation without breaking nearby identities, attributes, or speech style.

Three tracks, four tasks, one theme: remove a specific identity or concept while preserving everything around it.
Task 3Original concept unlearning from the earlier Genμ challenge, retained unchanged.
Track AFace identity unlearning with Arc2Face, CelebA, FFHQ, and ArcFace-based evaluation.
Track BSpeaker identity unlearning with prosody and attribute preservation for TTS / cloning models.

Challenge map

Each track is designed to test whether a model can forget the target while keeping the surrounding structure intact.

Legacy task 3

Concept unlearning

Keep the original benchmark as-is for the 2026 edition.

  • Base model: Stable Diffusion v1.4
  • Core methods: ESD, CA, FMN, FADE
  • Primary focus: erasing, retention, robustness
Track A

Face identity unlearning

Forget a target face identity while preserving neighbors and facial attributes.

  • Datasets: CelebA and FFHQ
  • Base model: Arc2Face
  • Metrics: forgetting, retention, leakage, geometry
Track B

Speaker identity unlearning

Remove a target speaker while keeping prosody, emotion, accent, and quality.

  • Model family: pretrained StyleTTS2
  • Evaluation: speaker, prosody, leakage, naturalness
  • Focus: disentangling identity from style
  • Metrics: FSA, RSA, Spk Similarity, spk-ZRF

New tasks in 2026

A1. Similar identity unlearning with retention of the nearest identities in ArcFace space.
A2. Identity unlearning with attribute preservation such as sunglasses, smile, beard, or hairstyle.
B1. Speaker identity unlearning with prosody and style preservation across diverse speakers.
B2. Speaker identity unlearning with speech attribute preservation for accent, emotion, and rhythm.

Datasets and models

Face track

Target identities are sampled from CelebA and FFHQ. The doc specifies Arc2Face as the starting model. ArcFace embeddings are used for similarity, leakage, and retention checks.

Speech track

The speech track uses a pretrained text-to-speech or voice cloning model as the base system. The release can include target-speaker samples, retained-speaker samples, and attribute labels for accent, emotion, rhythm, and style.

What stays from the earlier challenge

The original concept-unlearning benchmark stays live as Task 3 so teams can compare new identity-centric methods against the legacy setting. That gives the 2026 edition a direct bridge from concept forgetting to identity forgetting.
