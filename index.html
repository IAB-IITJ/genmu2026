Multimodal Identity Unlearning Challenge

The 2026 update expands the original Genμ concept-unlearning benchmark into a broader privacy-preserving suite co-located with the U&ME Workshop at ECCV 2026. Three tracks test whether generative models can forget a specific identity or concept without damaging everything around it.

Submissions are collected through three track-specific Google Forms. Each entry must provide a shareable Google Drive link to the submitted weights, a GitHub repository containing the complete code, and clear instructions for reproducing and testing the method.

How to submit

Use the Google Form for the selected track. A separate form is used for each track.

1. Weights

Upload the final unlearned weights or checkpoint to Google Drive and enable access for the organizers.

2. Complete code

Provide a GitHub repository containing training/unlearning code, inference code, environment files, and the exact commit or release tag.

3. Testing instructions

Document setup, commands, inputs, expected outputs, hardware requirements, and any track-specific preprocessing needed to reproduce the submission.

Challenge tracks

Face and speech participants may use any suitable generative model. Suggested checkpoints are starting points only; every submission must identify its exact model and checkpoint.

Task 1 · Face

Face Unlearning Track

Forget a target face identity while preserving neighboring identities and facial attributes.

  • Data: CelebA / FFHQ
  • Model choice: open
  • Suggested checkpoints: Arc2Face, InstantID, IP-Adapter-FaceID
Validation identitiesFace submission form — link pending
Task 2 · Speech

Speech Unlearning Track

Remove a target speaker while preserving linguistic content, prosody, accent, and speech quality.

  • Data: LibriTTS / LibriTTS-R
  • Model choice: open
  • Suggested checkpoints: StyleTTS2-LibriTTS, XTTS-v2
Validation speakersSpeech submission form — link pending
Task 3 · Concept

Concept Unlearning

The original benchmark: erase a visual concept while retaining adjacent concepts, utility, and robustness.

  • Reference setting: Stable Diffusion v1.4
  • Baselines: ESD, CA, FMN, FADE
  • Primary metric: ERR score

New sub-tasks in 2026

A1
Similar identity unlearning with retention of the nearest identities in ArcFace embedding space.
A2
Identity with attribute preservation — forget the face but keep attributes such as sunglasses, smile, or beard.
B1
Speaker unlearning with prosody — remove the voice identity while preserving pitch, energy, and rhythm.
B2
Speech attribute preservation — forget speaker identity while keeping accent, emotion, and style.
