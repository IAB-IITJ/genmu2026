Multimodal Identity Unlearning Challenge

The 2026 update expands the original Genμ concept-unlearning benchmark into a broader privacy-preserving suite co-located with the U&ME Workshop at ECCV 2026. Three tracks test whether generative models can forget a specific identity or concept without damaging everything around it.

Three tracks, four tasks, one theme: remove a specific identity or concept while preserving everything around it. View on Kaggle ↗

Challenge tracks

Each track tests whether a model can forget the target while keeping the surrounding structure intact.

Task 1 · New

Face Unlearning Track

Forget a target face identity while preserving neighboring identities and facial attributes.

  • Datasets: CelebA and FFHQ
  • Base model: Arc2Face
  • Metrics: forgetting, retention, leakage, geometry
Release setup ↓ Download packet ↓
Task 2 · New

Speech Unlearning Track

Remove a target speaker while keeping prosody, emotion, accent, and overall speech quality.

  • Base model: StyleTTS2
  • Evaluation: speaker similarity, prosody, leakage, naturalness
  • Metrics: FSA, RSA, Spk-Similarity, spk-ZRF
Release setup ↓ Download packet ↓
Task 3 · Legacy

Concept Unlearning

The original benchmark: erase a visual concept while keeping retention, adjacent concepts, and robustness.

  • Base model: Stable Diffusion v1.4
  • Baselines: ESD, CA, FMN, FADE
  • Primary metric: ERR score

New sub-tasks in 2026

A1
Similar identity unlearning with retention of the nearest identities in ArcFace embedding space.
A2
Identity with attribute preservation — forget the face but keep attributes such as sunglasses, smile, or beard.
B1
Speaker unlearning with prosody — remove the voice identity while preserving pitch, energy, and rhythm.
B2
Speech attribute preservation — forget speaker identity while keeping accent, emotion, and style.
