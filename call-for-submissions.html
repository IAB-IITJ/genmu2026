Challenge open · Call for submissions

How the 2026 challenge is structured

Teams can work on the Face Unlearning Track, the Speech Unlearning Track, the legacy concept benchmark, or all three. The challenge is built around forgetting the target without damaging the surrounding capability of the generator.

The challenge is open. Final submissions are due 1 July 2026; evaluation and results declaration are scheduled for 7 July 2026. Top-performing teams may be invited to co-author a research paper with the organizing team, provided their method surpasses the published baselines. Submit on Kaggle ↗

Track summary

Task 1 · New

Face Unlearning Track

Face identity unlearning using CelebA / FFHQ samples, Arc2Face, and ArcFace-based evaluation. Sub-tasks A1 and A2.

Task 2 · New

Speech Unlearning Track

Speaker identity unlearning with prosody and attribute preservation for TTS or voice-cloning systems. Sub-tasks B1 and B2.

Task 3 · Legacy

Concept Unlearning

Legacy concept unlearning on Stable Diffusion v1.4 with the original erasing-retention-robustness (ERR) setup.

Recommended submission shape

Method report

  • What is being forgotten and why that formulation was chosen
  • How retention is protected during unlearning
  • What data or prompts are used in the unlearning procedure
  • How the method avoids leakage to adjacent identities or concepts

Artifacts

  • Unlearned model checkpoint or inference wrapper
  • Short technical write-up (2–4 pages recommended)
  • Optional: ablations and qualitative output samples
  • Track-specific evaluation logs in the required format

Notes for participants

The face and speech tracks are intentionally more entangled than ordinary concept erasure. Expect similarity preservation, leakage checks, and attribute retention to matter as much as the forgetting score itself. Methods that aggressively erase the target at the cost of collateral forgetting will score poorly on the retention and geometry metrics.
