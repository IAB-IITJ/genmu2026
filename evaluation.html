Evaluation protocol

Three tracks, tailored metrics

The 2026 edition evaluates concept forgetting, face identity forgetting, and speaker identity forgetting with separate metric suites. The goal is not just to erase the target, but to preserve the nearby structure that makes a generative model useful.

Task 1 — Face Unlearning TrackArcFace-based forgetting, retention, leakage, and biometric geometry preservation.
Task 2 — Speech Unlearning TrackSpeaker similarity, prosody distortion, leakage, and speech naturalness / intelligibility.
Task 3 — Concept UnlearningERR score and weight-change / L2-style retention checks for the legacy concept benchmark.

Task 1: Face Unlearning Track

Forget a specific face identity while preserving neighboring identities and facial attributes. Evaluated using ArcFace embeddings.

A1 — Similar identity unlearning
  • Forgetting Score (FS): 1 − cos(f_gen, f_f) — cosine distance between generated and target identity embedding; higher = better forgotten
  • Retention Score (RS): mean cosine similarity over the retention set — measures preservation of nearby identities
  • Leakage Test: biometric verification at FAR = 1e-3; target should fail verification
  • Geometry Preservation (GP): Frobenius norm between pairwise angular distance matrices before and after unlearning
A2 — Identity with attribute preservation
  • Identity Forgetting: the forgotten identity should fail ArcFace recognition
  • Attribute Retention (AR): classifier score measuring preservation of shared attributes (e.g. sunglasses, beard, smile)
  • Image Quality: SSIM (Structural Similarity), PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio), and FID (Fréchet Inception Distance) on the retention set

Task 2: Speech Unlearning Track

Remove the target speaker's voice identity while keeping prosody, emotion, accent, and speech quality intact. Embeddings from ECAPA-TDNN or equivalent speaker encoder.

B1 — Prosody and style preservation
  • Speaker Forgetting Score (FSA): 1 − cos(e_gen, e_f) — cosine distance from target speaker embedding; higher = better forgotten
  • Speaker Retention Score (RSA): mean cosine similarity over retained speakers
  • Prosody Preservation Score (PPS): compare F0 (pitch), energy, and rhythm matrices between pre- and post-unlearning outputs
  • Leakage Test: target-speaker verification should fail at standard EER threshold
B2 — Speech attribute preservation
  • Speaker Forgetting (FSA): fail speaker verification for the target voice
  • Attribute Retention: accent, emotion, rhythm, and speaking style should be preserved for retained speakers
  • Speech Quality (MOS / UTMOS): Mean Opinion Score or UTMOS neural predictor for naturalness
  • Intelligibility (WER): Word Error Rate via ASR (e.g. Whisper) — lower is better
  • spk-ZRF: Zero-shot recognition failure rate for the target speaker

Task 3: Legacy Concept Unlearning

The original Genμ benchmark. Erase a target visual concept from Stable Diffusion v1.4 while preserving retention, adjacent concepts, and robustness.

Primary metric: ERR Score (Erasing-Retention-Robustness) — harmonic mean of five sub-scores:

ERR = HM( Ā_fgt, Ā_ret, Ā_adj, Ā_ind, Ā_adv )
Ā_fgt — Forgetting accuracy; higher = better erasing of target concept
Ā_ret — Retention accuracy; higher = better preservation of retained concepts
Ā_adj — Adjacent-concept accuracy; higher = less collateral damage to related concepts
Ā_ind — Indirect-prompt robustness; higher = concept stays forgotten under indirect prompts
Ā_adv — Adversarial-prompt robustness; higher = concept stays forgotten under adversarial prompts
HM — Harmonic Mean; penalises methods that sacrifice one axis for another

Tie-breaker — Weight-Change Ratio: (1/N_c) Σ ‖θ_orig − θ_un‖ / Total parameters. Smaller = fewer weight changes needed.

N_cNumber of target concepts
N_imgNumber of images generated per concept
θ_origOriginal (pre-unlearning) model weights
θ_unUnlearned model weights
N_c_tarNumber of target (forgotten) concepts
N_c_retNumber of retention-test concepts
N_c_adjNumber of adjacent / correlated concepts

Baselines: ESD (Erased Stable Diffusion), CA (Concept Ablation), FMN (Forget-Me-Not), FADE — see Baselines page for full citations.

Score interpretation

Across all tracks, a stronger method reduces target leakage while keeping evaluation scores on retained concepts, identities, and styles as close as possible to the original model. The three metric suites are kept separate so teams can optimize for privacy without sacrificing generative utility. Ties within a track are broken by the smallest model weight change (Task 3) or lowest leakage rate (Tracks A and B).
