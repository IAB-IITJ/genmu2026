Call for entries

How the 2026 challenge is structured

Teams can work on the legacy concept benchmark, the face identity track, the speaker identity track, or all three. The challenge is built around forgetting the target without damaging the surrounding capability of the generator.

Submission scopeOne or more tracks are welcome.
Primary deliverableA model or method that meets the track-specific evaluation protocol.
Core promiseForget identities, keep utility.

Track summary

Task 3

Legacy concept unlearning on Stable Diffusion v1.4 with the original erasing-retention-robustness setup.

Track A

Face identity unlearning using CelebA / FFHQ samples, Arc2Face, and ArcFace-based evaluation.

Track B

Speaker identity unlearning with prosody and attribute preservation for TTS or voice-cloning systems.

Recommended submission shape

Method report

  • What is being forgotten
  • How retention is protected
  • What data or prompts are used
  • How the method avoids leakage

Artifacts

  • Unlearned checkpoint or inference wrapper
  • Short technical write-up
  • Optional ablations and qualitative samples
  • Track-specific evaluation logs

Notes for participants

The face and speech tracks are intentionally more entangled than ordinary concept erasure. Expect similarity preservation, leakage checks, and attribute retention to matter as much as the forgetting score itself.

Updated for the 2026 edition. Legacy concept unlearning continues as Task 3, alongside two new identity-centric tracks in face and speech.
