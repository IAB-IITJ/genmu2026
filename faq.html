FAQ

Is the original Genμ challenge still part of this website?

Yes. The earlier concept-unlearning challenge is retained as Task 3 so teams can still benchmark against the legacy setting. The 2025 edition website is also linked in the navigation under "Genμ 2025 ↗".

What is new in 2026?

Two identity-centric tracks have been added: Task 1 (Face Unlearning Track) and Task 2 (Speech Unlearning Track). Each track has two sub-tasks focused on different preservation objectives.

How are the tasks ordered?

Task 1 is Face Unlearning, Task 2 is Speech Unlearning, and Task 3 is the legacy Concept Unlearning benchmark. The ordering reflects the two new identity tracks first, followed by the retained benchmark.

What data is used for the Face Unlearning Track (Task 1)?

Target identities are sampled from CelebA and FFHQ. Arc2Face is the base model, and ArcFace embeddings are used for evaluation, leakage tests, and retention checks.

What data is used for the Speech Unlearning Track (Task 2)?

The speech track is built around a target-speaker set and a retention set with diverse prosody, accents, and emotions. Candidate corpora include LibriTTS, VCTK, and LJ Speech. The base model is StyleTTS2, evaluated with ECAPA-TDNN for speaker verification.

Can teams submit to more than one track?

Yes. Submissions to one, two, or all three tracks are welcome. The leaderboard shows per-track rankings, and teams can focus on whichever track best showcases their method.

Where is the Kaggle challenge?

The live leaderboard and submission portal are at kaggle.com/competitions/gen-challenge-u-me-workshop-iccv-25. Upload the CSV produced by the evaluation script to see your score update in real time.

What matters most in evaluation?

Each track balances forgetting against retention. Removing the target identity or concept is necessary but not sufficient — preserving nearby identities, attributes, style, and output quality matters equally. Methods that erase aggressively at the cost of collateral damage will score poorly.

When are the challenge dates?

Exact dates for the 2026 cycle will be posted on the home page as soon as the submission window is confirmed. Winners will be announced at the U&ME Workshop at ECCV 2026. Follow the workshop website for updates.
